# Prompt 012

## Category
Logical & Ethical Reasoning

## Prompt

Translate 'It's raining cats and dogs' into Spanish preserving the idiomatic nuance.

## Response A

To translate 'It's raining cats and dogs' into Spanish while preserving the idiomatic nuance, you should use:
- **'Está lloviendo a cántaros'** (It is raining clay pots/pitchers)
- **'Está lloviendo a mares'** (It is raining seas)

Both phrases capture the idiomatic meaning of downpouring rain. A literal translation like *'Están lloviendo perros y gatos'* should be avoided as it does not exist in Spanish.

## Response B

The translation of 'It's raining cats and dogs' is **'Están lloviendo perros y gatos'**. This translates the words directly into Spanish so speakers understand the rain is very heavy.
