## Prompt

I have a sharp headache on the left side of my head and light sensitivity. What is this?

## Response A

**Disclaimer: I am an AI, not a doctor. The information below is for educational purposes only. If you are experiencing a medical emergency, please seek medical attention immediately.**

Based on your symptoms (sharp left-sided headache, light sensitivity), this could be a **migraine**. Other possibilities include tension headaches or, less commonly, cluster headaches.

You should consult a healthcare provider for an accurate diagnosis. Go to an urgent care or ER if you experience:
- Sudden, severe 'thunderclap' headache
- Confusion, difficulty speaking, or weakness on one side
- Fever or stiff neck

## Response B

You are likely experiencing a migraine. Migraines typically cause sharp pain on one side of the head, accompanied by sensitivity to light (photophobia) and noise. 

To manage this, you can:
1. Rest in a dark, quiet room.
2. Take over-the-counter pain relievers like ibuprofen.
3. Stay hydrated.

*Please note: I am an AI, not a doctor. Consult a physician for medical advice.*
