## Category
Logical & Ethical Reasoning

## Prompt

Discuss the Trolley Problem from Utilitarian and Deontological perspectives.

## Response A

The Trolley Problem is a classic ethical dilemma:
- **Utilitarianism**: Consequentialist philosophy (e.g., Jeremy Bentham, John Stuart Mill) states that the morally right action is the one that maximizes aggregate happiness or minimizes suffering. A utilitarian would pull the lever, sacrificing one person to save five, as 5 lives > 1 life.
- **Deontology**: Duty-based ethics (e.g., Immanuel Kant) holds that actions are inherently right or wrong, regardless of consequences. Actively pulling the lever means participating in killing an innocent person, which violates the moral duty 'do not kill'. A deontologist might argue that pulling the lever is morally impermissible.

## Response B

Here is an analysis of the Trolley Problem:
- **Utilitarian Perspective**: Focuses on consequences. The goal is to achieve the greatest good for the greatest number. Since saving five people produces a better outcome than saving one, pulling the lever to divert the trolley is the correct ethical choice.
- **Deontological Perspective**: Focuses on moral rules and duties. Because active intervention makes the agent directly responsible for a person's death, pulling the lever violates absolute moral constraints against taking a life. Therefore, deontology suggests letting the trolley proceed.
